SOFT ON
THE OUTSIDE.
DELIBERATE
EVERYWHERE ELSE.

Rhode does not scream. It does not have to. The brand identity is built on restraint with intention. Every touchpoint, from the packaging to the subject line of an email, carries the same quiet confidence. Blush pinks and warm nudes are not just aesthetic choices. They are a brand promise: this will feel good on your skin and in your hands.

The Playfair Display serif communicates something luxurious without being cold. The lowercase "rhode" logo is disarming. It says you do not need to try hard when the product does the work.

SKINCARE
SHOULD FEEL
LIKE SOMETHING
YOU WANT.

Rhode exists because skincare got complicated. Twelve-step routines. Lab-grade ingredients with names nobody could pronounce. Products that promised everything and felt like nothing. Rhode launched in 2022 with a simple counter-proposal: a small, curated range of products that actually work and feel luxurious to use.

Skincare is not a project. It is a moment you give yourself.
Rhode Brand Voice
We built what we actually wanted to use every day. Nothing more.

Voice and Personality
04 of 05

WARM.
KNOWING.
NEVER
TRYING HARD.

Rhode writes like your most put-together friend. Confident but not cold. It knows things. It shares them freely. It never lectures.

Rhode Does This
✓
Short sentences. Active voice. Confidence without explanation.
✓
Names shades like moments. Strawberry Glaze. Salted Caramel. Toast. You can taste them.
✓
Self-aware humor. "New flavour alert. Strawberries and cream. We know. We could not stop crying either."
✓
Writes to one person. Not a demographic. A specific human having a specific morning.
Rhode Never Does This
✗
Jargon. Peptides are mentioned but never explained like a textbook.
✗
Desperation. No countdown timers. No "LAST CHANCE" in all caps.
✗
Over-promising. "Your skin after a long weekend." Not "transform your skin in 7 days."
